The Intentional Spending Tracker is designed to help you take control of your finances by applying the 50/30/20 budgeting rule. Purpose of the App

The Intentional Spending Tracker is designed to help you take control of your finances using the simple and effective 50/30/20 budgeting rule. By categorizing your income and expenses into three straightforward categories—Needs, Wants, and Savings/Debt—you can easily manage your budget, stay on top of your spending, and work towards your financial goals.

What is the 50/30/20 Rule?

The 50/30/20 rule is a budgeting method that divides your income into three key categories:

  • 50% Needs: Essential expenses that you can’t live without. These include things like rent or mortgage payments, groceries, utilities, transportation, and insurance.
  • 30% Wants: Non-essential expenses that enhance your lifestyle. This category includes dining out, entertainment, shopping, and vacations.
  • 20% Savings/Debt: Financial priorities that ensure your future stability. This category covers emergency savings, retirement contributions, and paying off debt.

The beauty of the 50/30/20 rule is its simplicity and flexibility, making it an ideal budgeting strategy for various income levels and financial situations.

Add Income

1. Navigate to the Dashboard: After logging in, you’ll find yourself on the dashboard.

2. Click on ‘Add Income’: This will open a modal where you can input your income details.

3. Planned Amount: Enter the amount you expect to receive. This could be your salary, freelance payments, or any other sources of income.

4. Received Amount: This is the actual amount you’ve received. It’s important to update this field once the income is received to keep your budget accurate.

5. Save: Click the 'Add Income' button to save your entry. You’ll see your income listed along with a breakdown of your planned versus received amounts.

Add Expenses

1. Navigate to the Expenses Section: The dashboard allows you to add expenses under three categories—Fundamentals, Fun, and Future You.

2. Click on ‘Add Expense’: Choose the category (Fundamentals, Fun, or Future You) that best fits your expense.

3. Planned Amount: Input the amount you plan to spend in this category. Planning your expenses ahead helps you stay within your budget.

4. Spent Amount: This field should be updated with the actual amount you’ve spent in that category. Monitoring this helps you track if you’re overspending.

5. Save: After filling in the details, click 'Add Expense'. Your expenses will be listed under the appropriate category with both planned and spent amounts displayed.

Understanding the Sidebar

Total Income: This section shows the sum of all your received income. It’s essential to keep this updated for accurate budgeting.

Planned: This tab displays the total amount you’ve planned to allocate across all three categories—Fundamentals, Fun, and Future You. It helps you see at a glance if your budget aligns with the 50/30/20 rule.

Spent: Here, you can track your actual spending in each category. It’s an excellent way to monitor your spending habits and adjust as necessary.

Remaining: This section shows the remaining amount after accounting for all your expenses. Positive numbers indicate money left over, which you can allocate to savings or treat yourself, while negative numbers suggest a need to tighten your budget.
